Pot Sticker Stir Fry

Yield: 4 servings
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes

Ingredients

Stir Fry Sauce

  • 1 tbsp ginger (minced)
  • 1 tbsp garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Meat and Veggies

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1/2 head of napa cabbage
  • 8 oz chopped carrots

Toppings

  • 1/4 cup green onions

Instructions

  1. Fry the meat and carrots, brown the meat.
  2. Add the Stir Fry Sauce. Mix until fragrant.
  3. Add the cabbage. Cook until the cabbage is just wilted.
  4. Top each serving with 2 tbsp green onion.

Notes

  • You can add some shitake mushrooms for extra umami.
  • Pork also works quite nicely, but I use turkey to keep it lean. You could use tofu or seitan to make it Friday Friendly

Pickled Radishes or Turnips

Yield: 4 servings
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 20 minutes

Ingredients

Pickling Juice

  • 7 tbsp white wine vinegar
  • 2 tsp sherry vinegar
  • ⅛ cup white sugar
  • ¾ cup water
  • 1 ½ tsp salt

Veggies

  • 2 cups baby turnips or radishes, thinly sliced
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme

Instructions

  1. Put the Veggies and thyme in a mason jar
  2. Bring the pickling juice to a boil on the stove
  3. Pour the juice into the jar
  4. Let the container cool on the countertop, then place in the fridge.
  5. The radishes should sit overnight. Turnips should sit for at least two days.

Notes

The pickling juice amounts are kind of weird because I tried to get everything to fit neatly into a single mason jar. This recipe from NYT cooking inspired me, so you could just use their ingredients. However, I think the fresh thyme adds an important pizzazz to the recipe.

Dal Fry

Yield: 8 servings
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 40 minutes

Indian lentil soup

Ingredients

Dal (Split Peas)

  • 2 cup yellow split peas toor dal
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tsp turmeric

Whole Spices

  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp red chili flakes
  • 1/2 tsp asafoetida (hing)

Aromatics

  • 2 large onion finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup garlic minced
  • 1/4 cup ginger minced
  • 1/2 tsp green chili paste

Veggies and Dry Spices

  • 1 15 oz can tomato sauce
  • 1/4 tsp garam masala
  • 1/4 tsp chili powder
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander leaves (cilantro)

Instructions

    1. Cook the Dal in the instant pot: 8 minutes on high pressure, natural release
    2. Meanwhile, fry the Whole Spices in a pan with some oil until they become aromatic
    3. Add the Aromatics, fry for a few minutes
    4. Add the Veggies and the Dry Spices, cook for 8 minutes.
    5. Add the Dal and mix well. Let it sit for a few minutes to let the flavors blend.

Notes

This recipe was inspired by Cooking with Manali

Peanuts & Paneer: Thai Style

Yield: 6 servings
Prep Time: 40 minutes
Cook Time: 15 minutes
Total Time: 55 minutes

A Thai-inspired stir fry shrimp, fried paneer, and veggies.

Ingredients

Peanut Sauce

  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 6 tbsp lime juice, (juice of about 2 limes)
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger
  • 5 tbsp peanut butter, crunchy
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • ¼ cup fresh coriander leaves, (cilantro)

Proteins

  • 1 lb shrimp, cooked
  • 14 oz paneer, cubed (½ inch)

Veggies

  • 6 oz bean vermicelli noodles, cooked
  • ½ large English cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 4 medium radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium red bell pepper
  • 8 oz baby bella mushrooms, quartered

Instructions

  1. Combine all the Peanut Sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
  2. In a small pan, fry the paneer in your oil of choice. It should be nicely crispy! Set aside.
  3. Fry the mushrooms and shrimp. These ingredients released a lot of water, which I drained after cooking. Add to the bowl of paneer.
  4. Cook the vermicelli.
  5. Combine the cucumber, radish, red bell pepper, and coriander leaves in a bowl.
  6. You should have a total of 4 bowls, which is stupid and excessive, but it's what I did.
  7. Toss the mushrooms, shrimp, and paneer in the peanut sauce, reducing the number of bowls to 3.
  8. Serve by placing some vermicelli, then the shrimp + paneer mixture, then the veggies, on a plate.

Notes

Cooked bean vermicelli should look horrifying and totally inedible. My husband said "people should use this for haunted houses, not spaghetti"

Oat-browns

Yield: 2
Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 25 minutes

Fat bomb protein lactation pancake awesome.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 2 tbsp flaxseed
  • ¾ cup water
  • 50 g mozzarella cheese
  • 3 slices cooked bacon, crumbled
  • 2 eggs
  • oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Cook the oats and flaxseed in the microwave. (That's what the water is for).
  2. Meanwhile, fry up the bacon if you haven't got crumbles on hand. Leave the oil in the pan for frying.
  3. When the oats + flaxseed are done, mix in the mozzarella cheese and the bacon crumbles.
  4. Divide the mixture in two. Make two very thin pancakes. Spoon into a hot pan, like you would extra thick pancake batter.
  5. The pancakes should start getting really crispy, almost like hashbrowns.
  6. After a minute or two, flip and cook for another minute.
  7. Set the pancakes aside.
  8. Top each pancake with a fried egg.

Notes

If you're like "ok, eew, flaxseed, I'm not into that," recognize that it's a binding agent and your pancakes won't stick together as well. Also, it's a good galactogogue.
